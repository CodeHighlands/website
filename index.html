FAQ

Hackathon? What are we hacking?

The word Hackathon is a combination of the word "Hack" and "marathon". The definition of hack is to create something in a short period of time. A hackathon is a coding competition that's held for college and high school students. Each hackathon is 24-72 hours long. Code Highlands will be about 27 hours long.

Who's allowed to participate?

All High schoolers are welcomed with open arms! If you're a college student, you can help mentor the hackers!

What if I can't code?

Awesome! Code Highlands will be a great place to learn. We'll have a bunch of mentors at your disposal.

What should I bring?

Bring your school ID, laptop, your laptop charger, your cellphone, your cellphone charger, and any hardware you'll be hacking on! Power strips are recommended! If you plan on sleeping a sleeping bag is also recommended!

When can I start Coding?

You can start coding once the Opening Ceramonies are over. You can't start before, because that's cheating, and cheating isn't cool.
